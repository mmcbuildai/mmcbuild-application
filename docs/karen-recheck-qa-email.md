We've just shipped the full remediation loop in MMC Comply — the part where a non-compliant item gets sent to an engineer, they reply, you action it, and you re-run the check to confirm it's now clean. When you have five minutes, could you walk through it on one of your projects and let me know if anything feels off?

Here's the flow to try, on a project that's already had a compliance check with some non-compliant findings:

1. Engineer's reply is now visible.
   Open a compliance report and find an item you'd sent for remediation and had a response to. You should now see the engineer's written notes AND be able to download any file they attached — previously you only saw a status.

2. The new "Open Items" board.
   There's a link to it from the check page. Your non-compliant items are grouped by where they're up to: Open / Responded / Resolved / Waived. For each item you can:
   - Mark it resolved — either you've updated the drawings, or you've got a cert/evidence that covers it;
   - Waive it — accept it with a short reason you type in; or
   - Reopen it if you change your mind.

3. Re-check and see what changed.
   Once everything is resolved or waived, a "Ready to re-check" button appears. Hitting it runs a fresh compliance check (same as a normal run), and the new version shows you exactly what changed: what's now Cleared, what's Still open, and anything Newly introduced. Anything you waived carries across automatically, so you don't have to do it twice.

If any step throws an error or doesn't behave the way you'd expect, just reply with what you did and what happened — a screenshot is perfect — and we'll jump straight on it.
